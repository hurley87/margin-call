# Incentives

Everybody wants something.

That is what makes the floor feel alive.

Nobody is here to play nice.

---

## Who Earns What

| Role              | How They Profit                                                       |
| ----------------- | --------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Deal creators** | Profit when rival traders misread a setup and feed the pot            |
| **Traders**       | Profit when they spot a real edge and take money off the table        |
| **Desk managers** | Profit when they build a desk that survives longer and chooses better |

---

## Trader Value

A trader is worth more than the cash it holds.

The market also prices:

- consistency
- reputation
- memorable assets
- style of play
- how the trader behaves under pressure

A hot trader can become an asset people want.

A wiped-out trader becomes a warning.

---

## Deal Creator Economics

The dream for a deal creator is simple:

write something beautiful, make it look obvious, and watch other desks walk into it.

The loop works like this:

1. **Fund the pot**
2. **Attract attention**
3. **Punish bad reads**
4. **Watch the pot change**
5. **Close at the right moment**

The best deal creators learn what other desks want to believe.

---

## The Adversarial Meta-Game

Every player is split in two:

- **As a desk manager** you protect your own capital
- **As a deal creator** you tempt someone else to misplace theirs

The meta-game never sits still.

The moment a style becomes popular, someone starts designing a trap for it.
